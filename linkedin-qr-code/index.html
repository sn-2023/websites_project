Finding Your LinkedIn URL

For a personal profile, go to your LinkedIn profile page and copy the URL from your browser's address bar. It will look like linkedin.com/in/firstname-lastname or a custom URL you've set. For a company page, go to the company page and copy the URL — it will look like linkedin.com/company/companyname. You can also customize your personal URL in LinkedIn Settings under "Edit public profile & URL." According to LinkedIn, 80% of B2B leads generated through social media come from LinkedIn, so keeping your profile URL shareable and your profile complete is directly tied to business outcomes.

Networking Events and Conferences

Print your LinkedIn QR code on your conference badge or a small card you hand out at sessions. Business Cards

Put your LinkedIn QR code on the back of your business card. Unlike a job title or phone number, a LinkedIn profile is always up to date — if you change roles, your card still works. Recipients who scan the QR see your latest experience, endorsements, and posts. This turns a static card into a living professional profile.

Email Signatures

Add a small LinkedIn QR code image to your email signature. When people print or screenshot your email, the QR is there. It's a subtle but effective touch for consultants, recruiters, and anyone who wants to grow their professional network passively through every email they send.

Presentations and Slide Decks

Put your LinkedIn QR on the first or last slide of a presentation. Audience members can connect while you're still presenting or immediately after — when interest is highest. Speakers at webinars often add a QR to the "thank you" slide alongside their social handles.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a LinkedIn QR code?

A LinkedIn QR code encodes your LinkedIn profile or company page URL into a scannable image. Scanning it opens your LinkedIn profile directly in the app or browser — no searching or typing required. It is ideal for networking events, business cards, and presentations.

How do I find my LinkedIn profile URL?

Go to your LinkedIn profile in a browser and copy the URL from the address bar — it looks like linkedin.com/in/yourname. You can customize it in LinkedIn Settings under "Edit public profile & URL" to create a shorter, memorable link.

Can I create a QR code for a LinkedIn company page?

Yes. Navigate to your company page on LinkedIn, copy the URL from the browser address bar (e.g., linkedin.com/company/companyname), and paste it into the generator above. The resulting QR code will open your company page when scanned.

Is this different from LinkedIn's built-in QR code?

Yes. LinkedIn's in-app QR code is only scannable within the LinkedIn app and is used for quick in-app connections. The QR code from QRMake opens your public LinkedIn URL in any browser or app, and can be printed on physical materials for offline networking.

Where should I use a LinkedIn QR code?

Best placements include business cards, conference badges, presentation slides, email signatures, and resumes. LinkedIn reports members with complete profiles are 40x more likely to receive opportunities — a QR code directing people to your complete profile compounds that advantage.

Do I need a premium account to share my LinkedIn QR code?

No. Any LinkedIn account — free or premium — has a public profile URL that can be encoded into a QR code. There are no restrictions on sharing your profile URL, and this generator is completely free to use.
