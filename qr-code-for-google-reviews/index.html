Why Google Review QR Codes Work

Most customers are happy to leave a review — they just don't want to search for your business, find the review button, and navigate Google's interface. A Google Review QR code removes every step except writing the review. They scan, Google Maps opens with the review form ready, and they tap their star rating. The friction reduction alone can double or triple your review volume.

According to BrightLocal's 2023 Local Consumer Review Survey, 98% of consumers read online reviews for local businesses before making a purchasing decision. Google holds 81.7% of the search engine market globally (Statista, 2024), making Google Reviews the single most impactful review platform for local businesses. A Harvard Business School study found that a one-star increase in rating leads to a 5–9% revenue increase — and Google Reviews drives comparable impact for businesses of all sizes.

Step 1: Get Your Google Review Link

Open Google Maps and search for your business. Click on your business listing, then click "Write a review". Copy the URL from your browser's address bar — this is your direct review link. Alternatively, go to your Google Business Profile dashboard, find the "Get more reviews" section, and copy the short link Google provides. This link typically looks like: https://g.page/r/YOUR-ID/review.

Can't Find Your Review Link?

Search for your business on Google, click your listing, and look at the URL. You need the Place ID. Go to the Google Place ID Finder (search "Google Place ID Finder"), enter your business name, copy the Place ID, and construct this URL: https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID.

Step 2: Generate the QR Code

Go to the QRMake generator, paste your Google Review URL, customize the colors to match your brand, and download the QR code as PNG (for receipts and screens) or SVG (for printed materials like table tents and signage).

Step 3: Place It Where Customers See It

At the register/checkout: A small sign or sticker near the payment terminal catches customers right after a positive transaction — the best moment for a review.

On receipts: Print the QR code at the bottom of receipts with a short message like "Enjoyed your visit? Leave us a review!"

On table tents (restaurants): Place between the dessert menu and the check. Diners who just had a good meal are primed to review.

In follow-up emails: Include the QR code image in your post-purchase or post-visit email.

On packaging: E-commerce businesses can add a QR code to packaging inserts or thank-you cards.

Tips to Get More Reviews

Ask at the right moment. The best time is immediately after a positive interaction — when the customer compliments your service, when they receive their order, or when they express satisfaction. Don't wait until they've left and forgotten the experience.

Keep the ask simple. "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? Just scan this code — takes 30 seconds." People respond to specificity ("30 seconds") better than vague requests.

Don't incentivize reviews. Offering discounts or freebies for reviews violates Google's policies and can get your reviews removed. Simply making it easy is enough.

Respond to every review. When potential reviewers see that you respond to feedback (positive and negative), they're more likely to leave their own review. It signals that you actually read them.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I create a QR code for Google Reviews?

Find your Google Review link by going to Google Maps, searching for your business, clicking "Write a review", and copying the URL. Alternatively, go to your Google Business Profile dashboard and copy the short review link. Then paste that URL into QRMake, customize the QR code colors, and download as PNG or SVG. The QR code will take customers directly to your Google review form.

Where do I find my Google Review link?

You can find your Google Review link in three places: (1) Search your business on Google Maps, click "Write a review", and copy the browser URL. (2) Log in to your Google Business Profile dashboard — the "Get more reviews" section provides a short shareable link. (3) Use the Google Place ID Finder to construct a direct URL: https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID.

How do I get a short Google review URL?

Log in to your Google Business Profile at business.google.com. In the dashboard, look for "Get more reviews" or "Share review form". Google provides a short link in the format https://g.page/r/YOUR-ID/review. This is the cleanest URL to use in your QR code — it is shorter and produces a simpler, more reliable QR pattern than the full Maps URL.

Can I put a Google Review QR code on a receipt or invoice?

Yes, and receipts are one of the highest-converting placements for Google Review QR codes. Add the QR code to the bottom of printed or digital receipts with a short message like "Enjoyed your experience? Leave us a Google review — scan to go straight there." Customers who just completed a transaction are in a positive frame of mind and more likely to leave a review.

Does my business need to be verified on Google to get reviews?

Yes. Your business must have a verified Google Business Profile to receive Google Reviews. Unverified businesses can appear in Google Maps but reviews may not display prominently or count toward your rating. Verification is free and typically takes 1–7 days via postcard, phone, or email. Go to business.google.com to start the verification process.

How long does it take for a Google review to appear?

Most Google reviews appear within a few minutes to a few hours. However, Google's spam filter may delay or remove reviews that appear suspicious — including reviews from new Google accounts or reviews written from the same IP address as your business. Genuine reviews from real customers generally appear quickly and remain permanently.
